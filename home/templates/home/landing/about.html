WHO WE ARE

Recreating values is an acitivity of great minds that is channeled towards the transformation of africa from a third world continent to a first world continent through the recreation of lost societal values which are fundumental to the core of any nation building. Values are treasures that people hold dear and by recreating these values, we hope to refreshen and enliven them again in the african space so as to evoke a renewed interest and vigor in observing and practicing them

OUR MISSION

In Recreating values, we build a better society for our progeny. We hope to acheive this through inculcating the necessary values a great nation ought to have into the African peoples, most especially children and young adults who are still malleable. We also spark up conversations in various quarters by organising talks, symposia, and debates in order tp engage our people, children and adults in discussions on developing Africa. Small things when neglected crumble bigger things. These things may seem insignificant but they are the causative agents of change in any developed society. We also sensitise through our writings and community outreach, our doctors, lawyers, teachers, nurses, engineers, priests, pastors, farmers, traders, scientists, researchers, jounalists, police, military, politicians, authors, civil servants, bankers, artisans(bricklayers, carpenters, plumbers, electrcians, painters, artists), industrialists, actors, musicians etc that their professions and jobs are great and essential to the society and they should practice and live out the required values inherent in their profession in order to develop and create a healthy community of dignified leaders and gentlemen who are living up to their full potentials.

OUR VISION

In developed climes, there are certain operating values instrumental to their development. For example, they work hard are truthful and clean and so on. As a result of this, they have a serious working population, an equitable justice system and clean environnments, respectively amongst others. Obviously, the African nations are deficient in these values, which results in deriliction to duty, injustice and often squalid environments. The basic element of nation building is not the government but the citizens, the people. The duty of the government is simply to provide for thre citizens what they really need to work with. If Africa imbibes the core societal values of positive hardwork and excellence and shuns mediocrity,our growth will be at a geometric progression. Our different systems will be efficient: the justice system, education system, economic system, political system , health system and so on. Africans are intelligent and we can rule the world. Let all hands be on deck. So help us God
